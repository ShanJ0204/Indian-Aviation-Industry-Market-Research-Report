Executive Summary

The Indian aviation industry in 2025 is characterized by robust growth in passenger traffic and market size, alongside significant profitability challenges for airlines. The market, valued at $14.47 billion in 2024, is projected to reach $40.81 billion by 2033, representing a strong CAGR of 12.21%[458].

Key trends include the dominance of Low-Cost Carriers (LCCs) controlling 69% of capacity[526], substantial government initiatives like UDAN with 625 routes connecting 90 airports[421], and a strong focus on infrastructure development targeting 200 airports by 2025[417].

Market Dynamics

Passenger Traffic Trends

India's aviation sector demonstrates robust growth with domestic passenger traffic increasing by 11.04% year-on-year in February 2025, reaching approximately 14 million passengers[453].

International passenger traffic shows even stronger recovery, with 41.3% increase compared to pre-Covid levels, reaching 28.09 million passengers in the first 10 months of FY2025[453].

Low-Cost Carriers have fundamentally reshaped Indian air travel, with IndiGo leading the market with 63.7% share in February 2025[453].

The success of LCCs has made flying accessible to a broader segment, stimulating overall market growth and challenging traditional full-service models.

The UDAN scheme has been pivotal in democratizing aviation, connecting Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities while expanding India's airport network from 74 in 2014 to 159 in 2024[421]. The Union Budget 2025-26 proposes extending the scheme to 120 new destinations, targeting an additional 40 million passengers over the next decade[420].

Sustainability Initiatives

National SAF Framework

India has set an indicative blending target of 1% Sustainable Aviation Fuel by 2027 [285]. The ISCC-NABCB partnership enables Indian certification bodies to perform SAF audits according to international standards.

India SAF Alliance

Launched in June 2025, this collaborative effort involves airlines, energy companies, and research institutions to accelerate SAF production and adoption[266].

Airline Sustainability Programs

IndiGo

Committed to 10% SAF by 2030, 78% fuel-efficient A320neo fleet by mid-2025, and 25% carbon reduction per passenger kilometer by 2030[251].

Air India

MoU with CSIR-IIP for SAF research and development, focusing on indigenous feedstock and production pathways[252].

SpiceJet

First in India to operate TaxiBot at Delhi Airport, electric tarmac coaches, and initiatives to reduce single-use plastics[164].

CORSIA Compliance & Carbon Offsetting

India will join the mandatory phase of CORSIA starting in 2027 [269]. Indian airlines operating international flights will need to monitor, report, and verify emissions, purchasing carbon offsets for emissions above baseline levels.

The DGCA has issued Civil Aviation Requirements for CORSIA, applicable to operators exceeding 10,000 tonnes of CO2 emissions annually from international flights[270].

Implementation Timeline

Mandatory offsetting phase for Indian operators commences January 1, 2027, requiring comprehensive emissions monitoring and reporting systems.

Financial Health

Industry Profitability Challenges

Despite robust passenger growth, the Indian aviation industry faces significant profitability challenges. ICRA projects net losses of Rs 20-30 billion for FY2025 and FY2026 [560].

Key Cost Pressures

  • • ATF prices: 35-40% of operating expenses
  • • Rupee depreciation against USD
  • • Intense competition and fare wars
  • • High fixed-cost business model
